🔒
Your Keys. Your Wallet. Always.
VanityGen is built on a simple principle — we never see, store, or touch your private keys. Ever.
🖥️
Everything runs on YOUR device
Every wallet generated here — Solana or EVM — is created entirely inside your browser using your device's own CPU. No data is sent to any server during generation. The cryptography runs locally in a Web Worker thread, completely offline.
🙈
Admins cannot see your private key
Private keys are generated in your browser and displayed only to you. They are never transmitted to our servers, never logged, and never stored anywhere outside your screen. Not even the developers of this tool can access them — it is technically impossible.
📖
Open and verifiable
This is a single HTML file. Anyone can right-click → View Page Source and read every line of code. There are no hidden scripts, no external API calls during wallet generation, and no obfuscated code. You can verify this yourself in under a minute.
🔑
Real cryptography, not fake
Solana wallets use Ed25519 keypairs via the industry-standard TweetNaCl library. EVM wallets use secp256k1 — the exact same algorithm used by MetaMask, Ledger, and every major Ethereum wallet. Your generated address is fully compatible with Phantom, Solflare, MetaMask, Rabby, and any other wallet app.
⚡
Why vanity addresses are safe
A vanity address is just a regular wallet whose address happens to start or end with characters you chose. The private key is generated with the same randomness as any other wallet. The pattern search only selects which keypair to show you — it does not weaken the security of the key in any way.
✅ BEST PRACTICES
•Copy your private key immediately after generation and store it in a password manager or offline.
•Never share your private key with anyone, including support staff. No legitimate service will ever ask for it.
•Generate wallets on a trusted device. Avoid using public Wi-Fi or shared computers.
•Once you close or refresh the page, your private key is gone from this app. Make sure you have saved it first.
